Description

Themed food is popular at cinemas, in entertainment experiences and at theme parks. It's only natural that we should drink Butterbeer at the Wizarding World of Harry Potter, or indulge in a Moroccan feast while watching Casablanca at Alamo Drafthouse. We are still ourselves in these settings, however, and the actors shooting scenes don't necessarily get to enjoy the culinary creations made for their respective films (culinary creations which might not even be real). Bridge the gap with PROPS, a dining experience that includes costumes, production design and dinner, of course, for a film that doesn't really exist but which takes place in an twentieth-century canvas tent. Think English Patient, Moonrise Kingdom, Tarzan. It's a jumping-off point for discussion and analysis, of how we feel about eating when we're pretending or trying to be different people, in a narrative we haven't personally experienced.
