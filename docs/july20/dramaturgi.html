Dramaturgies for Dystopias: Live Performance in a Pandemic and Beyond

Dinner • 7 spots available

Date

Monday, July 27 at 7:00 PM EST

Description

As a theatre major and actor, I've been trying to reconcile what it means to study live performance in this moment. For obvious reasons, Broadway was one of the first things to be shut down as New York responded to coronavirus. It is likely one of the last things that will reopen. Many performance artists are out of jobs, as the concept of “live” has been threatened. But there are also people out there who are showing us that perhaps the idea of “live” is not threatened but that it has shifted. This take on ~dinner theatre~ is inspired by Jeremy O. Harris' masterclass with NYTW ("Dramaturgies for Dystopias 101"). If I learned anything from this class, it is that those that will rise from this are those that will innovate. Perhaps there is something positive to gain from the forced creativity that we must all apply to our old ways right now. Join me as we watch a little "play" that I wrote for the screen that involves the live typing out of text. We can then discuss what live performance looks like without bodies, what live performance looks like right now, and how live performance will be fundamentally changed forever!
