Description

Currently there are people in jail convicted of an act that is no longer a crime; simultaneously, there are thousands of people currently building intergenerational wealth through the exact same act: selling weed. These same people with felony convictions--when released--will be unable to enter the cannabis industry due to their record. How did we get here? The War on Drugs and its relation to mass incarceration in the U.S. runs deep, and it's why we still struggle with addiction recovery today. The Controlled Substances Act of 1971, the wars in Afghanistan, and Big Pharma are all related. It's the logic behind criminalizing addiction and drugs that help with addiction (like psychedelics, weed, and more). Ending the WoD is one of the most important steps in the move to abolish prisons. So, roll yourself a joint, and let's smoke and talk about it.
