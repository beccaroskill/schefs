Description

In the past few decades, vegetarianism and veganism have become increasingly popular in Western countries. Reasons for personally abstaining from meat include religion, ethics, environmental concern, and health consciousness. But what legislation oversees the wellbeing of animals? What is its historical precedent? This conversation will look toward various world histories of animal welfare in culture and law, and then delve into the present state of animal welfare in the US.

What to Prepare

The documentary Dominion does a really good job of investing animal cruelty in factory farm, but it is very graphic. The conversation would benefit from some background knowledge of such farming practices, since I'll mostly be talking about the legislative context of the animal product industry.
