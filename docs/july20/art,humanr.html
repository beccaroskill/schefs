Art, Human Rights and Social Justice: Creative paths to healing

Lunch • 7 spots available

Date

Wednesday, July 15 at 2:00 PM EST

Description

Join us for a timely conversation tackling issues related to art, human rights and notions of transformative justice. Both of us have studied and experimented many ways in which art can be used as a powerful tool to heal individuals and communities, working with artists who deserve to make their voices heard. We believe that connecting creative disciplines to social justice is particularly relevant to the times we are living: the arts can help us ask questions about means to heal collectively in societies built on gender, racial and class hierarchies. What kind of solutions can be bring to alleviate suffering? Offer healing to communities that have tremendously suffered? How art can be a powerful tool to push for and to achieve greater equality and justice? Our talk will be centered around those questions. We will start by explaining how our own experiences have shaped the way we think about those questions. We will then open the floor to hear from you!

About the Host

Noa and Hanna just graduated from the Dual BA between Columbia University and Sciences Po Paris. Hanna studied Political Science and Philosophy at Columbia. She particularly focused on justice, and transformative forms of justice. She founded the Digital Abolitionist with two abolitionist friends, an educational platform on abolition and she organized a virtual exhibition featuring works by artists that have been directly impacted by the criminal legal system. She is actively working on death row advocacy campaign. Noa studied Human Rights and Psychology at Columbia. She is the producer of the first Columbia edition of the International Human Rights Art Festival which occurred last November, featuring student artists whose work was centered around issues of social justice. Noa was one of the winners the Columbia Human Rights Essay Contest for her research paper on « Art Therapy for Refugees ».
