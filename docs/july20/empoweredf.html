Dinner & drinks • 7 spots available

Description

Internship culture at many universities is currently predicated upon secrecy and distrust. We contribute to this toxicity when we gate-keep crucial internship-related knowledge or deliberately uphold an air of secrecy around resources and opportunities. Moreover, in addition to our complicity as individuals, institutions and employers are often also implicated in this toxic culture through unpaid opportunities and other ways they keep resources and opportunities inaccessible. Join this discussion on pre-professional culture in college and on strategies we can use to remind ourselves and others that there is enough success to go around — and that by aiding one another in navigating these institutions, we are able to help deconstruct the underlying white supremacist ideals of ego and individualism that permeate our society.
