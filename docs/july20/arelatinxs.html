Are Latinxs or Black Artists Shows at Big Institutions Tokenism or Milestones?

Tea • 7 spots available

Date

Wednesday, July 22

Description

Come drink some tea and have a conversation on representation and lack thereof in the world of fine art. How do race based and gender based divisions affect artists and where do we draw the line between tokenism and milestones? The Smithsonian’s “Our America: Latino Presence in American Art” show will serve as our jumping off point. Should we get rid of race based divisions in art? Are art shows based on gender or race divisions rather than aesthetic divisions beneficial or detrimental for their respective communities? And what repercussions does this have for the art world?

About the Host

Sofia Grosso is a practicing artist based in New York and Palm Beach. She is an advocate for diversity in all fields, and especially the decolonization of the fine art world. Her work centers around explorations of color theory and her experience in the United States growing up as a first generation Colombian American. She explores symbols of latin "otherness", such as tropical fruits and maps of Latin America. She loves hot chocolate, cold tea, and warm soup.
