Coping with Comedy: How our generation processes pain with humor

Tea • 7 spots available

Date

Saturday, July 25

Description

2020 has been emotionally exhausting to say the least. Yet through it all we’ve been making comedy! In the past 10 years or so people have started to use comedy to uplift, challenge, satirize, and dissect the present moment. This feels vastly different from the way past generations have used this medium. Comedy’s history is full of content that belittled, oppressed, and scrutinized. Only recently, have younger comedians (and honestly younger people) been able to use comedy as a way to share in collective experience. Is it our constant creation of content or our welcoming and reshaping of PC culture (which is really just inclusive and non racist culture). I want to talk about it! Is it really just a coping mechanism?We’ve done something cool with comedy and it is worth pointing out!
