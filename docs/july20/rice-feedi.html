Description

One of the most unifying experiences for the Asian American community is eating rice. From basmati to jasmine, the short and sticky to the long and delicate, we all have our own type of rice and the stories to go along with it. In the first half, we will all share a thought about rice: why scorched rice is the ultimate snack food, that time you forgot to cook the rice before mom came home, or just your favorite way to eat rice (desserts! noodles! alcohol?). Then, we will broaden our conversation to Asian food in college. How has your experience been with the food in dining halls? Do you miss the food back at home? Can you find adequate substitutes? How has quarantine made this better or worse? This is a time to think and talk about food and the Asian American identity.
