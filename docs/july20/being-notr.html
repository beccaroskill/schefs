Description

In light of recent events, viral videos of racist confrontation, publicized murder of black people at the hands of authorities, and international protest in response. Non-Black people are coming to terms with the realities of racism that implicates them. Realization that taking the passive stance of being "not-racist" is no longer enough; therefore, opening up conversation for anti-racism work. In this event I hope to discuss the difference between the two, and the active work of anti-racism.

About the Host

Born in Haiti, and raised in Miami Florida, Kwolanne Felix is a sophomore at Columbia College studying the History of the African Diaspora. As the creator of the Inclusion and Diversity Task Force, she seeks to advocate for the needs of marginalized students through collaboration and community organizing. Kwolanne's passion for international politics, human rights, and gender equality has been expanded through her experiences working in communications with the Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) and the National Organization for Women. Moreover, her time as a research assistant for the Political Science Department of Columbia, investigating human rights and gender issues in Africa politics, has fortified her love for advocacy on the international scale. In her free time, she writes a column for the Columbia Spectator reflecting on nuanced intersectional issues of her community. After receiving her bachelors degree, Kwolanne will pursue a Law Degree (JD) focusing on international legal studies.
