Description

Come learn how to make a simple and tasty beef bolognese sauce! Hosted in a 121st St Kitchenette, this comprehensive cooking lesson will teach you how to make one of the MOST BASIC pasta sauces, which will be the highlight of your next dinner party (or personal meal, if you choose to store it in the fridge, which I do all the time). I will also go over safe cutting techniques with you (for the garlic and onions). This experience is ideal for the meat-eating college student; a lot of the work is done by the stovetop, you can babysit it while doing homework (or watching Netflix, let's be real), and is easily re-heatable as needed to feed you and your friends. You will leave with a printed copy of my ~*coveted*~ recipe, an ingredients list, and a container of sauce. Please bring a tupperware container if you can!

About the Chef

My name is Sophia, and I'm a sophomore at Barnard majoring in English hoping to concentrate in Creative Writing. I moved to New York City from New Jersey when I was 12, and have lived here since. Cooking runs in the family— I get a lot of my recipes from my parents and grandparents. I work as a Shift Lead Barista at a coffee shop in the High Line Hotel called Intelligentsia, where you can catch me on weekends on our coffee bus as I try to catch up on my readings for my classes in between rushes.
