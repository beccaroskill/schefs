Description

Come enjoy an Italian-inspired, seasonal-themed dinner in my dorm kitchen! The meal will begin with a selection of crostini served on local bread. From there, we will enjoy a salad comprised of locally-grown, winter greens with a garlicky dressing. Then, using one of my favorite seasonal ingredients as the star of my meal -- mushrooms -- I will be crafting a porcini mushroom tomato sauce, which will be paired with handmade fettuccine. Finally, we’ll end the night with a caramel flan. My aim in putting together this selection is to highlight an example of a no-waste meal, as well as to promote the benefits of choosing seasonal ingredients (not only are they more nutritious, but are more often locally-sourced and thus less harmful to the environment) and to share my love of Italian culture. You are welcome to help with any of the cooking stages (it’s always fun to use the pasta maker) or you can just sit back and delight in the flavors!

About the Chef

Hello! I’m currently 19 years old and hail from Brooklyn, NY. Growing up, food was always a massive part of my family life and I often found myself helping out in the kitchen, watching in awe as my relatives whipped up new concoctions. In high school, I became really interested in food systems issues and started spending my summers on farms, hoping to more intimately connect myself to what I consumed every day and to expand my knowledge of industry standards. From there, a passion ignited and I started cooking more, taking food history classes, and seeking out new cuisines/agricultural practices -- suffice it to say that I love all things food. Beside that, I am also really into filmmaking, art, travel, current events, and sustainability. Come and cook with me!
